About

Wide taxonomy first, then richer guided use

CogBias begins with the Wikipedia cognitive-bias list as a wide seed taxonomy, then reshapes that material into a teaching-first site with paths, self-audits, prompt kits, theory notes, and deeper editorial treatment of especially important bias pages.

Why this architecture works

The site can now grow in three directions at once: broader coverage through the imported catalog, deeper coverage through hand-authored upgrades, and stronger usability through path pages and procedural tools.

Files to edit first

  • data/site.json for branding, featured entries, taxonomy copy, and countermoves.
  • data/biases.json for generated coverage.
  • data/editorial_enrichments.json for richer hand-authored entry sections.
  • data/entry_teaching_modules*.json and data/assessment_bank*.json for flagship-page pedagogy and the mixed assessment runner.
  • data/learning_paths.json, data/self_checks.json, data/prompt_kits.json, and data/theory_articles*.json for the guided layers.
  • scripts/import_wikipedia_biases.py for refreshing the seed catalog.

What stayed, what changed

The point was to keep the feel of LogFall while changing the intellectual center of gravity.

Keep

  • Static GitHub Pages architecture
  • Single structured data source
  • Searchable index and category pages
  • Simple client-side filtering

Rewrite

  • Shift the taxonomy from fallacy families to Wikipedia-seeded bias categories and patterns
  • Tune page framing around distorted judgment instead of public argument structure
  • Reserve illustration slots now so the later image set lands cleanly
  • Use generic fallbacks for broad coverage while keeping richer hand-authored pages for core biases

Add

  • Pattern pages alongside category pages
  • Source links back to the Wikipedia seed taxonomy
  • Image placeholders on detail pages
  • A more extensive imported catalog with future editorial layering
  • Mixed scenario assessment and flagship-page teaching modules

Defer

  • Original illustration production
  • Printable worksheets and classroom packets
  • Long-form theory essays
  • Assessment workflows until the core catalog is more settled

Seed taxonomy and broad coverage are drawn from Wikipedia's List of cognitive biases, then editorially reshaped into a teaching-first reference.
