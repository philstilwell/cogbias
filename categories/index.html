Taxonomy

Categories for recurring judgment failures

These clusters are less about memorizing labels and more about seeing which repair moves belong together.

How To Use Them

Ask which kind of distortion is active, then look for the smallest process change that would make the judgment more reliable.

Attention & Salience

Biases in this cluster overweight what is vivid, recent, threatening, or easy to recall.

2 biases

What is grabbing attention here that may not deserve this much weight?

Belief Protection

These biases stabilize the stories we already prefer, especially when identity or commitment is involved.

2 biases

What would this person have to see before they would seriously revise the current view?

Estimation & Prediction

These biases distort numerical judgment, forecasting, calibration, and first-pass estimates.

2 biases

What anchor, confidence level, or forecast frame is quietly steering the estimate?

Choice & Commitment

Biases here make us cling to defaults, past investments, and emotionally protected options.

2 biases

Which option feels safer merely because it is familiar, costly to exit, or already underway?

Social Judgment

These biases simplify people too quickly by overreading character, traits, or first impressions.

2 biases

What situational explanation or hidden context might this snap judgment be ignoring?

Evidence & Sampling

These biases distort what counts as representative evidence and which cases even make it into view.

2 biases

What base rates, missing cases, or unobserved failures are absent from the sample?

Memory & Retrospection

These biases rewrite the past after outcomes arrive, making learning look cleaner than it was.

2 biases

Are we remembering the decision process fairly, or only remembering the ending?
