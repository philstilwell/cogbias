Learning Path

Evidence And Explanation

Biases that corrupt sampling, explanation, and the interpretation of evidence before a confident belief hardens.

8 biases Foundational 50 min

Use It When

Use this path when you suspect that the apparent evidential picture is itself distorted.

Track

Start here if you want the core labels, the most reusable distinctions, and the first debiasing moves.

Guiding Question

What makes a weak sample or flattering story feel like a strong explanation?

By the end of this path

  • Differentiate selective evidence handling from genuinely stronger explanation.
  • Notice when rates, denominators, and missing cases have been displaced by a cleaner story.
  • Get comfortable generating rival hypotheses before reading further.

How to study it

Work the pages in order, then loop back and compare which distortions happened earliest, which ones protected the first impression, and which ones interfered with later learning.

Confirmation bias

The tendency to notice, seek, and remember evidence that supports the story you already prefer more readily than evidence that threatens it.

Motivated reasoning

The tendency to judge the quality of a decision mainly by how things turned out rather than by the quality of the reasoning under the uncertainty that existed at the time.
