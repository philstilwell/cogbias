People Judgment

A path for social perception, hiring, leadership, conflict, and the fast trait inferences people make about one another.

6 biases Foundational 45 min

Use It When

Use this path when a discussion is drifting from behavior into identity, motive, or character verdict.

Track

Start here if you want the core labels, the most reusable distinctions, and the first debiasing moves.

Guiding Question

How do snap impressions about people become stronger than the evidence available?

By the end of this path

  • Catch the fastest routes from behavior to trait verdict.
  • Separate social impression from evidence about motive, character, or capacity.
  • Build a stronger baseline before entering harder conflict and identity-heavy cases.

How to study it

Work the pages in order, then loop back and compare which distortions happened earliest, which ones protected the first impression, and which ones interfered with later learning.

Implicit bias

The underlying attitudes and stereotypes that people unconsciously attribute to another person or group of people that affect how they understand and engage with them.
