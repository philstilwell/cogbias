Cognitive Bias

Hindsight bias

The tendency, after an outcome is known, to see it as having been more obvious or predictable than it actually was beforehand.

Memory & Retrospection Structured process Postmortems & learningForecasting & planning

Default mental move

Rewrite the old decision problem as if the ending had always been visible.

What it distorts

It destroys calibration and learning by making past uncertainty vanish.

Common trigger

Postmortems, political analysis, investing, sports talk, and any outcome with a strong public narrative.

First countermove

Use a decision journal or timestamped notes to recover what was actually known at the time.

Mechanism

Once the ending is in place, memory edits the uncertainty out of the original situation. The path to the outcome becomes narratively smoother than it was in real time.

Signals You Are In It

  • After the event, people claim the signs were obvious without having documented them earlier.
  • Original uncertainty is remembered as much narrower than it was.
  • Alternative outcomes stop feeling live once the actual one happens.

Why This Category Matters

These biases rewrite the past after outcomes arrive, making learning look cleaner than it was.

Are we remembering the decision process fairly, or only remembering the ending?

Counter It

From detection to intervention

Bias work is less about slapping on a label and more about changing what happens next in the decision process.

Spot It

  • Ask what specific forecast was recorded before the outcome.
  • Check whether the person can distinguish 'makes sense now' from 'was predictable then'.
  • Look for timeline compression and certainty inflation.

Slow It

  • Keep written forecasts and assumptions.
  • Review the information set as it existed then, not what is known now.
  • Include alternative outcomes that were genuinely plausible at the time.

Reframe It

  • Treat past uncertainty as real even when the ending now feels tidy.
  • Ask what would have happened to your narrative if the outcome had gone differently.
  • Use hindsight as a prompt for model revision, not for self-flattering inevitability stories.

Related biases

These nearby patterns often travel with hindsight bias or get confused with it.

Outcome bias

The tendency to judge the quality of a decision mainly by how things turned out rather than by the quality of the reasoning under the uncertainty that existed at the time.
