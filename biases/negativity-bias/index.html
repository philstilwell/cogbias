Cognitive Bias

Negativity bias

The tendency to give bad news, threats, criticism, and losses more psychological weight than equally sized positives.

Attention & Salience Quick reset Media & politicsTeams & management

Default mental move

Overweight what could go wrong and underweight quiet competence or improvement.

What it distorts

It can make the whole environment look worse, riskier, or more hostile than the total evidence supports.

Common trigger

Conflict, uncertainty, public criticism, and feedback environments that highlight errors.

First countermove

Force a count of what is going right before deciding how diagnostic the negative signal really is.

Mechanism

Potential harms command attention quickly because organisms that miss threats pay steep costs. That makes negative information sticky, memorable, and behavior-shaping.

Signals You Are In It

  • One bad interaction outweighs many ordinary or good ones.
  • The discussion keeps circling around dangers without proportioning them.
  • Neutral events are encoded as mildly negative by default.

Why This Category Matters

Biases in this cluster overweight what is vivid, recent, threatening, or easy to recall.

Counter It

From detection to intervention

Bias work is less about slapping on a label and more about changing what happens next in the decision process.

Spot It

  • Ask whether the current emotional weight matches the actual rate or magnitude of the problem.
  • Check whether positive base-line performance has disappeared from view.
  • Notice whether a criticism is being treated as more informative than equivalent praise.

Slow It

  • Count positive, neutral, and negative cases separately.
  • Review trend lines instead of the single worst episode.
  • Distinguish serious risks from ordinary friction.

Reframe It

  • Ask what a neutral observer would score here.
  • Treat vivid bad news as a signal to inspect, not an automatic verdict.
  • Make room for the possibility that the system is mixed rather than globally bad.
