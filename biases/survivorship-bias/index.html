Cognitive Bias

Survivorship bias

The tendency to learn from the visible winners while overlooking the invisible failures that dropped out of view.

Evidence & Sampling Team protocol Research & evidenceForecasting & planning

Default mental move

Infer quality from the surviving sample without asking who is missing.

What it distorts

It makes risky paths look safer and strategies look stronger than they are.

Common trigger

Entrepreneurship stories, hiring narratives, investing, and institutional mythmaking.

First countermove

Ask what relevant failures are absent from the observed sample and why they are absent.

Mechanism

Success stories are easier to observe, celebrate, and circulate. Failed cases disappear, leaving the remaining sample badly distorted.

Signals You Are In It

  • The discussion focuses on people or projects that made it through the filter.
  • Failure data is unavailable, ignored, or treated as irrelevant.
  • Advice is drawn from admired outcomes without denominator information.

Why This Category Matters

These biases distort what counts as representative evidence and which cases even make it into view.

What base rates, missing cases, or unobserved failures are absent from the sample?

Counter It

From detection to intervention

Bias work is less about slapping on a label and more about changing what happens next in the decision process.

Spot It

  • Ask who did the same thing and did not survive to be counted.
  • Check whether the sample was filtered by success before the analysis began.
  • Look for missing attrition, dropout, or failure rates.

Slow It

  • Define the full reference population before analyzing the visible winners.
  • Search intentionally for failed comparators.
  • Report rates for entrants, not just for survivors.

Reframe It

  • Treat success stories as incomplete evidence, not as complete lessons.
  • Ask what the invisible cemetery of similar attempts would say.
  • Remember that visibility is often the consequence of success, not proof of replicable quality.
